## Top 3 of the day

1. **Google Quantum Echoes (arXiv:2506.10191, Nature Oct 2025)** — <https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.10191> — Google Quantum AI demonstrates the first claimed verifiable quantum advantage using OTOC measurements on their Willow chip. Unlike random circuit sampling, OTOC expectation values are verifiable (same result on a different quantum computer or natural quantum system) AND are beyond known classical simulation capacity (Google's claim). Paves way for Hamiltonian learning in NMR. **Dequantization unverified — skeptical framing required.**

2. **Optimal decoding by density matrix propagation (arXiv:2606.14455, June 12)** — <https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.14455> — Paris-Saclay/CEA group (Benois, Cussenot, Misguich, Sangouard, Hansenne) introduces density matrix propagation as a benchmark for optimal QEC decoding. Practical decoders fall 5× short of optimality on complex codes; key bottleneck is a small fraction of syndrome histories dominating logical error rate at low physical error rates. Directly relevant to trend-001; 5th independent group.

3. **End-to-end learning of quantum control (arXiv:2606.27907, June 26)** — <https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.27907> — Zhong et al. replaces the iterative simulate-then-optimize quantum control paradigm with end-to-end ML on a latent dynamical manifold; reduces computational overhead and improves scalability in noisy environments. 5th independent org for trend-002 (AI-for-quantum watch area).
